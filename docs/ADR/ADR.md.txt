Decision: Model AssessmentStrategy as a Value Object in the MVP.

Reason: It has no independent identity or lifecycle. It exists only as part of a Campaign.

Future trigger for refactoring: If TAO introduces reusable assessment strategy templates, versioning, or independent management, promote AssessmentStrategy to an Entity or Aggregate Root.

Title: AI Resume Screening is a Core Domain Module

Decision: Treat AI Resume Screening as a first-class module in TAO Acquire rather than as a utility under Campaigns.

Reasoning:

It has its own business workflow and lifecycle.
It encapsulates AI parsing, matching, scoring, and ranking.
It will evolve independently (multiple AI models, re-screening, explainability, confidence scores).
It is a key differentiator of TAO compared to traditional ATS systems.


Title: Resume Screening is the Core Capability of TAO Acquire

Decision:
Model Resume Screening as a primary domain capability rather than treating Resume Matching as a simple utility.

Reasoning:
Resume Screening encompasses parsing, AI analysis, match scoring, explanation generation, recommendations, confidence scoring, and candidate selection. Resume Matching is one step within that broader workflow.


Title: Introduce Resume Import Job as a first-class domain concept.

Decision: Resume ingestion will be modeled as an Import Job owned by a Campaign. Uploaded files are processed asynchronously, structured candidate data is persisted, and original files are retained only according to the organization's retention policy.

Reasoning: This supports large uploads, progress tracking, retries, partial failures, auditing, and future connector-based imports while keeping storage costs under control.

I actually think this is becoming one of the core aggregates of TAO Acquire, alongside Campaign. It models the real business process of ingesting resumes much better than treating uploads as a simple file attachment.




Title: Hybrid Resume Matching Engine

Decision:
Resume matching will use a hybrid approach:

AI extracts and normalizes hiring requirements from the Job Description.
AI parses and normalizes resume content.
A deterministic scoring engine calculates the match percentage using configurable rules and weights.
AI generates a human-readable explanation and confidence level.

Why?

Transparent and explainable scoring.
Consistent results for identical inputs.
Easy to tune business rules without changing prompts.
Recruiters can understand and trust why a candidate matched or didn't.
Easier to test and validate than relying on an LLM to produce a single percentage.


AI-First Configuration

Decision

Every configurable workflow in TAO Acquire should be proposed by AI first.

The recruiter should review and approve or modify the proposal rather than configuring it manually from scratch.

Applies to

Job Profile
Resume Matching Strategy
Screening Rules
Assessment Strategy
Candidate Invitation Templates
Future Hiring Recommendations

Reasoning

Reduces recruiter effort.
Keeps human oversight.
Improves consistency across campaigns.
Makes TAO feel like an AI hiring assistant rather than a configurable ATS.





Title: Candidate and Resume are separate domain concepts.

Decision: Model Candidate and Resume as separate entities.

Reasoning:

A candidate can submit multiple versions of a resume.
The same candidate may apply to multiple campaigns.
Resume screening is campaign-specific, while candidate identity is organization-wide.
This avoids duplicating candidate information across resumes.


Title: AI-First Domain Model

Decision:
TAO Acquire's domain model will be centered around AI-assisted hiring concepts (Job Profile, Hiring Strategy, Resume Screening) rather than traditional ATS concepts alone.

Reasoning:

AI is the primary value proposition of TAO.
Recruiters review and approve AI-generated artifacts rather than configuring everything manually.
The data model reflects the business workflow, not just CRUD operations.
This keeps AI outputs as first-class business assets that can be audited, refined, and reused.


Title: Introduce Application as a Core Aggregate in the Recruitment Lifecycle

Decision:
A Candidate is independent of any campaign. The relationship between a Campaign and a Candidate is represented by an Application.

Reasoning:

A candidate can apply to multiple campaigns.
Each application has its own resume, screening result, assessment, and hiring outcome.
The application becomes the lifecycle object that progresses through screening, assessment, and hiring.
This models real-world recruitment systems and avoids duplicating candidate data.



Title: Tenant-First Database Design

Decision: Design the database starting with Organization as the root of tenancy. Every business entity in TAO Acquire will belong to exactly one organization, either directly or transitively.

Reasoning:

Multi-tenancy is a core architectural requirement.
Prevents cross-tenant data leakage.
Simplifies authorization and query filtering.
Makes future scaling and tenant isolation straightforward.


ADR-00X – Abstract Resume Storage

Decision

Resume storage will be abstracted behind a storage provider.

For the MVP, resumes will be stored in SQL Server.

Future implementations may use Azure Blob Storage or other object stores without changing the domain or application layers.

Reasoning

No Azure costs during MVP.
Simpler deployment.
Clean migration path.
No dependency on cloud infrastructure.
Storage mechanism remains an Infrastructure concern.
